Student Intern

UNSW Tax and Business Advisory Clinic
Sep 2025 - Nov 2025

Reflection

This role was pivotal in bridging the gap between academic theory and real-world client interaction. Beyond the technical aspects of resolving PAYG issues and using Xero, the true value lay in developing empathy and communication skills. Conducting client interviews taught me how to translate complex tax concepts into accessible language for individuals in need. It made me realise how many people are quietly struggling behind numbers and forms. They are not just cases with tax issues but real life of people. Through this experience, I've come to see why it's called a clinic rather than an office. For me, it feels less like processing paperwork and more like offering a small piece of care to someone who's already exhausted. Even though we can't fix their whole life, helping them untangle a tax problem can remove one heavy burden, and in that moment it feels like we're offering a small beam of light into a pretty dark situation.
